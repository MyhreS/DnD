     BECOMING A HUNTER DEEPCALLER...
     AS A LEVEL 1 CHARACTER
     • Gain all the traits in the Core Hunter Deepcaller Traits table.
     • Gain the Hunter Deepcaller's level 1 features, which are listed in the Hunter Deepcaller Features
     table.

     HUNTER DEEPCALLER CLASS FEATURES
     As a Hunter Deepcaller, you gain the following class features when you reach the speci ed Hunter
     Deepcaller levels. These features are listed in the Hunter Deepcaller Features table.

     LEVEL 1: ELDRITCH COMPREHENSION
     Through occult rituals, you have expanded your mind beyond mortal limits, gaining the ability to
     comprehend eldritch passages and speak words no sane creature was meant to understand.
     These forbidden words allow you to read and perform Rites.

     See at the end of this class description for the rules on Rite-performing. The information below
     details how you use those rules with Hunter Deepcaller Rites, which appear in Appendix B “The
     Book of the Deepcaller” And Appendix C “Hushed Whispers”.

     Whispers. You know two Whispers of your choice. Whispers are lesser fragments of forbidden
     knowledge that are not written in the Book of the Deepcaller and must be memorized.
     Whenever you gain a Hunter Deepcaller level, you can replace one Whisper you know with
     another Whisper of your choice.
     When you reach Hunter Deepcaller levels 4 and 10, you learn another Whisper of your choice, as
     shown in the Whispers column of the Hunter Deepcaller Features table.
     Strains. The Hunter Deepcaller Features table shows how many Strains you have to perform your
     Rites of Levels 1–5. The table also shows the level of those Strains, all of which are the same
     level. You regain all expended Strains when you nish a Short or Long Rest.
     For example, when you are a level 5 Hunter Deepcaller, you have two Level 3 Strains. To perform
     a Level 1 Rite, you must spend one of those Strains, and the Rite is performed as a Level 3 Rite.
     The Book of the Deepcaller. Your Book of the Deepcaller contains the forbidden passages
     needed to perform Hunter Deepcaller Rites of level 1-5. You do not prepare these Rites. Any
     Hunter Deepcaller Rite in your book is available for you to perform, provided you have a Strain of
     a high enough level to perform it.
     As your Strain level increases, your mind becomes capable of comprehending deeper and more
     dangerous passages within the book. You can perform any Hunter Deepcaller Rite of a level equal
     to or lower than your current Strain level.
     For example, when your Strain level is 3, you can perform Hunter Deepcaller Rites of Levels 1, 2,
     and 3, but not Rites of Levels 4 or 5.
     Rite Performing Ability. Intelligence is the Rite Performing ability for your Hunter Deepcaller
     Rites.
     Performing a Rite. Unless otherwise stated, performing a Rite requires you to hold a Book of the
     Deepcaller in one hand and speak the Rite's words aloud. Each time you perform a Rite, you
     su er 2 Madness, unless a feature or e ect says otherwise.
     Performing a Whisper. Whispers are an exception to the normal rules for performing Rites.
     Performing a Whisper requires only a hushed recitation of its words. You do not need a Book of
     the Deepcaller to perform a Whisper, and performing a Whisper does not cause you to su er
     Madness unless an e ect says otherwise.
     Break the Limits of Your Mind. If you perform a Rite using a Strain level higher than your current
     Strain level, you su er Madness equal to your maximum Sanity immediately after the Rite is
     performed. This is not recommended.

     LEVEL 1: FRACTURING MIND
     Every time you level up su er 2 Madness and permanently gain 1 max Sanity.

     LEVEL 2: BRACED MIND
     As an Action, you steady your mind through an esoteric rite, forcing forbidden knowledge back
     into shape.
     When you do so, you su er 1 Madness and regain a number of expended Strains equal to half
     your maximum number of Strains, rounded up.

LEVEL 2: VAILED TRUTH
You no longer su er Madness from performing Level 1 Rites unless an e ect speci cally says so.

LEVEL 3: THE HUNTER ZEALOT PRESTIGE CLASS
At 3rd level, you may enter the Path of the Zealot Prestige Class, abandoning the traditional path
of the Deepcaller.
If you choose this Prestige Class, you gain no further features from the Hunter Deepcaller class.
Instead, whenever you would gain a Hunter Deepcaller feature, you gain the corresponding
feature from the Path of the Zealot Prestige Class.
You lose all Hunter Deepcaller features gained before entering this Prestige Class unless a Zealot
feature speci cally says otherwise.
The Path of the Zealot Prestige Class is detailed after the Hunter Deepcaller class description.
A Prestige Class is an alternative progression path that replaces your existing class path with a
specialized set of abilities.

LEVEL 3: OPENED MIND
As a Bonus Action, you can establish a telepathic link between yourself and up to eight willing
creatures you can see within 60 feet.
The link lasts for 1 round (the start of your next round). While linked, each creature may
communicate telepathically with the others. When the link is established, each linked creature
may contribute once to the conversation. The order is determined by the participants themselves.
Once a creature has nished communicating, the next creature may speak.
This communication occurs outside the normal ow of time and does not consume Actions,
Bonus Actions, Reactions, or movement.
Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you nish a Short or Long Rest unless you
expend a Strain (no action required) to restore your use of it.

LEVEL 4: ABILITY SCORE IMPROVEMENT
You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat (see chapter 4) or another feat of your choice for
which you qualify. You gain this feature again at Hunter Deepcaller levels 8, 12, and 16.

LEVEL 5: ENDURED TRUTH
You no longer su er Madness from performing Level 2 Rites unless an e ect speci cally says so.

LEVEL 6: OPENED MIND COMBATANT
When you use your Opened Mind feature, you may choose one creature within 60 feet that you
can see. The creature must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw against your Rite Save DC.
On a failed save, you glimpse fragments of its immediate future and perceive its intentions before
they unfold.
For the duration of 10 rounds, the chosen creature has Disadvantage on attack rolls against you,
and you have Advantage on attack rolls against it.

LEVEL 9: ACCEPTED TRUTH
You no longer su er Madness from performing Level 3 Rites unless an e ect speci cally says so.

LEVEL 10: FRAGMENTS OF A ELDRITCH MIND
You have Resistance to Mind damage, and whenever a creature deals Mind damage to you, that
creature takes the same amount of damage that you take.

LEVEL 11: FORBIDDEN REVELATION
Your studies uncover a fragment of knowledge that few minds can endure. The Book of the
Deepcaller begins to reveal hidden passages that were always there, but impossible for you to
perceive until now.
Choose one Level 6 Rite. This Rite becomes one of your Forbidden Revelations.
You can perform this Rite once without expending Strain. Once you perform it in this way, you
cannot do so again until you nish a Long Rest.

     THE DEEPCALLERS RITES

     TYPES OF RITES
     Each Rite has a type. The types are listed in the Types of Rites table. These categories help
     describe Rites but have no rules of their own, although some other rules refer to them.

     LONGER PERFORMING THAN ONE ACTION
     Certain Rites require more Actions to perform. While you perform a Rite with a Performing of two
     or more Actions, you must take the Rite action on each of your turns until the requirement is met,
     and you must maintain Concentration (see further down) while you do so.
     If your Concentration is broken, the Rite fails, but you don't expend a Strain. To perform the Rite
     again, you must start over.

     RANGE
     A Rite’s range indicates how far from the performer of the Rite the Rites e ect can originate, and
     the Rites description speci es which part of the e ect is limited by the range.
     A range usually takes one of the following forms:
     Distance. The range is expressed in feet.
     Touch. The Rite’s e ect originates on something, as de ned by the Rite, that the performer of the
     Rite must touch within their reach.
     Self. The Rite is performed on the performer self, or emanates from them, as speci ed in the Rite.
     If a Rite has movable e ects, they aren't restricted by its range unless the Rite’s description says
     otherwise.

                                   A Rites's duration is the length the Rites persists after it is performed. A duration typically takes
                                   one of the following forms:
                                   Concentration. A duration that requires Concentration follows the Concentration rules. (see
                                   further down).
                                   Instantaneous. An instantaneous duration means the Rites's outcome appears only for a
                                   moment and then disappears.
                                   Span. A duration that provides a span speci es how many rounds the Rite lasts. While span Rite
                                   that you perform is ongoing, you can dismiss it (no action required) if you don't have the
                                   Incapacitated condition or it is a Summoning Rite.

                                   CONCENTRATION
                                   Some Rites and other e ects require Concentration to remain active, as speci ed in their
                                   descriptions.
                                   If the e ect's creator loses Concentration, the e ect ends. If the e ect has a maximum duration,
                                   the e ect's description speci es for how many rounds the performer can concentrate on it. The
                                   creator can end Concentration at any time (no action required).
                                   The following factors break Concentration.
                                   Another Concentration E ect. You lose Concentration on an e ect the moment you start
                                   performing a Rite that requires Concentration or activate another e ect that requires
                                   Concentration.
                                   Damage. If you take damage, you must succeed on a Constitution saving throw to maintain
                                   Concentration. The DC equals 10 or half the damage taken (round down), whichever number is
                                   higher, up to a maximum DC of 30.
                                   Incapacitated or Dead. Your Concentration ends if you have the Incapacitated condition or you
                                   die.

                                   RITE EFFECTS
                                   The e ects of a Rite are detailed after its Duration entry. These e ects describe exactly what the
                                   Rite does, often ignoring the normal laws of nature. Any outcomes beyond those e ects are
                                   determined by the DM. A Rite's e ects typically involve targets, saving throws, Rite attacks, or a
                                   combination of the three.

                                   TARGETS
                                   Most Rites require you to choose one or more targets. A Rite's description speci es whether it
                                   targets creatures, objects, areas, or something else.
                                   A Clear Path to the Target. To target something with a Rite, you must have a clear path to it. A
                                   target behind Total Cover cannot be targeted unless the Rite states otherwise.
                                   Targeting Yourself. If a Rite targets a creature of your choice, you may choose yourself unless
                                   the Rite speci es a hostile creature or a creature other than yourself.
                                   Areas of E ect. Some Rites a ect an area rather than a speci c target. The Rite's description
                                   speci es the shape of the a ected area, which is typically one of the following: Cone, Cube,
                                   Cylinder, Emanation, Line, Sphere. A creature or object within the area is a ected as described by
                                   the Rite.
                                   Awareness of Being Targeted. Unless a Rite produces an obvious e ect, a creature is unaware
                                   it was targeted. E ects such as lightning, explosions, or summoned horrors are obvious. More
                                   subtle e ects, such as reading thoughts or in uencing the mind, typically go unnoticed unless the
                                   Rite states otherwise.
                                   Invalid Targets. If you perform a Rite on a creature or object that cannot be a ected by it, the
                                   Rite has no e ect on that target. Any Strain spent to perform the Rite is still expended.

                                   If the Rite normally allows a saving throw and has no e ect on a successful save, an invalid target
                                   appears to have succeeded on its saving throw. Otherwise, you perceive that the Rite had no
                                   e ect.

          COMBINING RITE EFFECTS
          The e ects of di erent Rites stack while their durations overlap. However, the e ects of the same
          Rite do not stack with themselves. If the same Rite a ects a target multiple times, only the most
          potent e ect applies. If multiple instances are equally potent, the most recent e ect applies. For
          example, if two Deepcallers perform the same Rite on the same target, the target bene ts from
          only one instance of that Rite unless the Rite speci cally states otherwise.




          HUNTER ZEALOT
          Most Deepcallers seek power through forbidden study, reading from the Book of the Deepcaller
          and calling upon many Patrons. A Zealot abandons that path. Through blood, re, and devotion,
          the Zealot o ers the book to a single eldritch being and becomes its living weapon.
          A Zealot does not search for many truths. A Zealot serves one Truth.

          LEVEL 3: BURN THE BOOK
          When you enter the Hunter Zealot Prestige Class, your Book of the Deepcaller is destroyed,
          burned, consumed, and claimed by your new Patron.
          You lose all Hunter Deepcaller features gained before entering this Prestige Class unless a Zealot
          feature speci cally says otherwise. You gain no further features from the Hunter Deepcaller class.
          Instead, whenever you would gain a Hunter Deepcaller feature, you gain the corresponding
          feature from the Hunter Zealot Prestige Class.
          You can no longer perform Rites of Level 2 or higher. You also lose all Rites of Level 2 or higher
          that you knew or had prepared.
          You retain your Rite Performing ability, Rite Save DC, and Rite Attack modi er.

          LEVEL 3: CHOSEN OF ONE PATRON
          Your new Patron becomes the source of your Zealot features.
          You no longer require a Book of the Deepcaller to perform Whispers. The words of your Patron are
          carved into your mind, blood, and esh.

          LEVEL 3: ZEALOT WHISPERS
          You retain the Whispers you knew before entering this Prestige Class.
          In addition, when you prepare Whispers, you can choose from both normal Whispers and Level 1
          Hunter Deepcaller Rites. A Level 1 Rite prepared in this way becomes a Zealot Whisper for you.

          A Zealot Whisper:
          does not require a Book of the Deepcaller,
          does not expend Strain,
          does not cause you to su er Madness,
          cannot be performed using Higher-Level Strain,
          uses your Rite Save DC and Rite Attack modi er as normal.

     LEVEL 3: STRAIN OF DEVOTION
     You no longer use Strain to perform Rites. Instead, your Strain becomes a weaponized expression
     of your devotion.
     Your Strain progression and maximum Strain level continue to improve as shown in the Hunter
     Deepcaller table. However, you can spend Strain only on Zealot features.
     You gain the following ways to spend Strain:
     Patron's Blow. Once per turn, when you hit a creature with a weapon attack or Eldritch Strike,
     you can expend one Strain to deal extra Eldritch Power damage to the target.
     The extra damage equals 1d8 per level of the Strain expended.
     Patron's Ward. When you take damage, you can take a Reaction and expend one Strain to
     reduce the damage by 1d8 per level of the Strain expended + your Rite Performing ability
     modi er.
     Patron's Step. As a Bonus Action, you can expend one Strain to teleport up to 10 feet per level of
     the Strain expended to an unoccupied space you can see.
     This movement does not provoke opportunity attacks.

     LEVEL 5: RITE AND BLADE
     You can attack twice, instead of once, whenever you take the Attack Action on your turn.
     In addition, when you use your Action to perform a Whisper, you can make one weapon attack as
     a Bonus Action.

     LEVEL 6: FANATICAL FOCUS
     Your devotion shields your mind from hesitation, fear, and pain.
     When you fail a saving throw, you can expend one Strain to reroll the saving throw. You must use
     the new roll.
     If the new roll succeeds, you can immediately move up to half your Speed without provoking
     opportunity attacks.

     LEVEL 10: ZEALOUS PRESENCE
     As a Bonus Action, you unleash a command lled with the authority of your Patron.
     Choose up to eight creatures of your choice within 60 feet that can see or hear you. Until the start
     of your next turn, each chosen creature has Advantage on attack rolls and saving throws.
     Once you use this feature, you cannot use it again until you nish a Long Rest, unless you expend
     one Strain to use it again.

LEVEL 20: ABSOLUTE UNION
As a Bonus Action, you allow your Patron to fully manifest through your body. This transformation
lasts for 5 rounds, until you drop to 0 Hit Points, or until you end it early as a Bonus Action.
Once you use this feature, you cannot use it again until you nish a Long Rest.
When you enter this form, you su er 20 Madness.
While transformed, you gain the following bene ts:
Patron's Form. Your body becomes a living vessel for your Patron. Your eyes turn black like the
abyss, blackened veins spread across your esh, impossible shadows cling to your body, bone
spines tear through your skin, and spectral limbs writhe around you as if guided by a will beyond
your own. When you transform, each other creature within a 30-foot-radius Sphere centered on
you must make a Wisdom saving throw against your Rite Save DC.
On a failed save, a creature takes 8d10 Mind damage and su ers 6 Madness. On a successful
save, a creature takes half as much damage and su ers 2 Madness.
Each other creature within 120 feet that can see your transformation unfold, but is outside the 30-
foot-radius Sphere, su ers 2 Madness.
Eldritch Resistance. You have Immunity to Mind damage and Resistance to Eldritch Power
damage.
Unshaken Vessel. You cannot be Frightened, Charmed, Paralyzed, or Stunned.
Devoted Assault. The rst time you hit a creature on each of your turns with a weapon attack or
Eldritch Strike, the target takes an extra 4d8 Eldritch Power damage.
Lashing Tendrils. As an Action, you command the spectral limbs of your Patron to lash out.
Choose up to three creatures you can see within 30 feet of you. Each target must make a Strength
saving throw against your Rite Save DC.
On a failed save, a target takes 6d10 Eldritch Power damage and gains the Restrained condition.
On a successful save, a target takes half as much damage and is not Restrained.
A Restrained creature can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the
condition on itself on a success.
You cannot use Lashing Tendrils again while a creature is Restrained by this feature.
Tendril Reprisal. When a creature within 15 feet of you hits you with an attack, you can take a
Reaction to lash out with a spectral tendril. The creature takes 3d10 Eldritch Power damage and
must succeed on a Strength saving throw against your Rite Save DC or have the Restrained
condition until the end of its next turn.
Unbroken Vessel. If you would drop to 0 Hit Points while this transformation is active, you can
expend one Strain to drop to 1 Hit Point instead.
If you drop to 0 Hit Points and do not use this bene t, the transformation ends.
