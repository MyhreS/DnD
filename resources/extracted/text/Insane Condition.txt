Insane Condition
(Transcribed from resources/pdf/appendices/Insane Condition.pdf)

Trigger. When your current Madness equals or exceeds your Max Sanity, you gain the
Insane condition.

Ending the Condition. The Insane condition ends immediately when your current Madness
is reduced below your Max Sanity.

Madness Die. While Insane, you have a Madness Die. The Madness Die is an 8-sided die
with three Blank faces, three Star faces, and two Eye faces.

Unstable Violence. Once per turn, when you take the Attack action or perform a harmful
Rite, choose your target or area as normal. Before resolving the attack or Rite, roll the
Madness Die.

Star. Resolve the attack or Rite normally.

Blank. The attack or Rite turns against your nearest ally instead. If more than one ally is
equally near, you choose which one is targeted. If no ally is within range, you must spend
available movement to bring an ally within range or reach, if possible. If no ally can be
targeted after this movement, the attack or Rite turns inward and targets you instead.

Eye. For one moment, you understand what you are about to do. You may either end your
turn immediately with no effect, or roll the Madness Die again. If the second roll is another
Eye, the vision breaks you: roll your Sanity Die, suffer that much Mind damage, and gain 1
Madness.

Cracked Perception. While Insane, you have Advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks
and Intelligence (Eldritch Knowledge) checks made to notice unnatural things, hidden
entities, dream-architecture, impossible movement, or occult distortions.

NOTE (app integration): Per the DM, what the Insane condition implies mechanically is a
table secret — the app must show only that a hunter IS Insane (Madness >= Max Sanity),
never these mechanics. Same for the "Lost" transformation result.
